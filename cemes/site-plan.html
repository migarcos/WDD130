What Is a Sealant? Sealants are used for a variety of applications in the building, construction, and remodelling industries. Most notably, sealants such as polyurethane, silicone, acrylic, and polymer are used as grouting and insulating materials that fill gaps and waterproof structures to prevent damages such as cracks and leaks. As a form of insulation, sealants also prevent air flow through building joints (e.g., windows and door frames) to regulate temperatures inside structures. Typically, sealants also have a certain amount of flexibility to allow for some structural movement and prevent damages. In addition to serving a practical purpose, gap sealants also add to the aesthetic appeal of a structure by hiding minor imperfections that occur during the construction process. Sealant is an inexpensive and easy to apply solution that can boost the functionality and aesthetic of any structure while adding long-term value and stability. What Is a Polyurethane Sealant? There are different configurations of polyurethane sealants that are currently available on the market. Each one is appropriate for various uses and applications, so always be sure to read the label carefully. With the accelerated upgrading of the construction industry, prefabricated buildings have shown a rapid development trend in recent years. As the first line of defense for external wall panel joint waterproofing, the performance of prefabricated building sealants is directly related to the waterproof effect of the project and it has attracted more and more attention. There are many types of construction sealants on the market, according to the different basic polymers, they can be roughly divided into: silicone sealant (SR), polyurethane sealant (PU), and silane-modified polyether rubber (MS polyurethane sealers). Polyurethane sealant (Pu sealant) is mainly composed of polyurethane rubber and polyurethane prepolymer. Polyurethane sealants are generally divided into two basic types, one-component and two-component One-component Pu sealer is moisture curing type, the one-component Pu sealer is convenient for construction, but curing is slower. Two-component Pu sealant is reaction curing type.; the two-component Pu sealant has the characteristics of fast curing and good performance, but it needs to be formulated during use, and the process is more complicated. Silicone sealant has good compatibility with silicone structural sealant, and is widely used in weather-resistant sealing of building curtain walls.
